Charcoal? Nope, Just My Mom's Eyebrow Pencil

Proof that you don't need fancy tools to start making art.

October 28, 2025

by Crystal

I didn't have art supplies, but I had determination — and my mom's eyebrow pencil was good enough. Little did I know, that was the start of a long love affair with art.

The Spark of Curiosity

I first discovered my love for drawing back when I was in 6th grade. One ordinary afternoon, I stumbled upon a YouTube compilation of realism drawing tutorials. Out of curiosity, I tried following along — and to my surprise, I immediately loved the results.

At the time, I didn’t have any fancy art materials yet. So, instead of charcoal pencils, I “borrowed” my mother’s eyebrow pencil to mimic that deep, smudgy blackness that charcoal usually creates. It was a little unorthodox, but it worked! My favorite subjects were realistic eyes and lips — something about capturing tiny details fascinated me.

“My days were filled with smudged fingertips, stacks of sketch paper, and the quiet satisfaction of finishing each piece.”

When the pandemic hit, I found even more time to practice. What started as a simple hobby became my daily creative escape. I began exploring anime-style drawings of my favorite characters before gradually returning to realism. With every sketch, I challenged myself to capture emotions and shadows more precisely.

Eventually, I built enough confidence to draw full portraits. I often used black-and-white photos from Pinterest as references, since I mainly worked with graphite and charcoal pencils. My days were filled with smudged fingertips, stacks of sketch paper, and the quiet satisfaction of finishing each piece.

I would proudly show my drawings to my brother, who became my biggest supporter — and, to be honest, my main art supply sponsor. Looking back, I realize that those small moments of curiosity and resourcefulness led me to discover something that still inspires me today.

I never planned to become an "artist". I just wanted to try something new one afternoon. But that small experiment with an eyebrow pencil turned into a creative and meaningful journey that shaped who I am today.
